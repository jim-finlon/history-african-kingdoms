## Detailed Chronological Periods

### Late Antiquity / Early Period (~300-700 CE)

**Active Major Polities:**
- **Aksum** — Height of power under Ezana (c. 321-360), conversion to Christianity, conquest of Kush, invasion of Yemen under Kaleb (525). Controlled Red Sea trade.
- **Nubian Kingdoms** — Nobatia, Makuria, and Alodia emerge from post-Meroitic chaos. Conversion to Christianity (c. 540s-580). Nubian archers repel Arab invasions (641-652). Baqt treaty established.
- **Ghana (Wagadu)** — Early formation period. The Soninke state begins to consolidate control over trans-Saharan gold-salt trade routes. Earliest Arabic references appear later, but oral tradition and archaeology suggest roots in this period.

**Parallel World Events:**
- Fall of the Western Roman Empire (476)
- Rise of Islam (622+)
- Arab conquest of Egypt (642)
- Tang Dynasty in China (618-907)
- Sassanid Persia and its fall (651)

---

### Middle Period (~700-1100 CE)

**Active Major Polities:**
- **Ghana Empire** — Apogee. Al-Bakri describes a powerful, wealthy state (c. 1068). Controls gold trade; religiously pluralistic court. Almoravid pressure (c. 1076) begins decline.
- **Kanem** — Founded around Lake Chad by the Sayfawa dynasty (c. 700s-800s). Gradual expansion and Islamization.
- **Makuria** — Height of Nubian Christian civilization. Cathedral culture, wall paintings, diplomatic relations with Abbasid Caliphate and Fatimid Egypt.
- **Swahili Coast** — Urbanization accelerates. Towns like Shanga, Manda, and early Kilwa develop. Indian Ocean trade brings Persian Gulf, Indian, and Chinese goods. Islam adopted by coastal elites.
- **Aksum** — Decline and transformation into the Zagwe period. The kingdom contracts; capital shifts south.
- **Yoruba (Ife)** — Ife develops as a major urban and cultural center. The famous terracotta and bronze portrait heads date from this and the following period.
- **Igbo-Ukwu** — The remarkable 9th-century burial site in Igbo territory demonstrates sophisticated bronze-casting and long-distance trade connections.

**Parallel World Events:**
- Abbasid Caliphate (750-1258)
- Carolingian Empire in Europe
- Viking Age (793-1066)
- Song Dynasty in China (960-1279)
- First Crusade (1096)

---

### High Period (~1100-1400 CE)

**Active Major Polities:**
- **Mali Empire** — Founded by Sundiata Keita (c. 1235). Expands to dominate the entire western Sahel. Mansa Musa's hajj (1324) makes Mali famous across the Islamic world and Europe. Timbuktu, Djenne, and Gao flourish as centers of trade and learning.
- **Zagwe Dynasty / Solomonic Ethiopia** — Zagwe dynasty (c. 1137-1270) builds the rock-hewn churches of Lalibela. Solomonic "restoration" under Yekuno Amlak (1270). Amda Seyon's conquests (1314-1344) establish Ethiopian dominance over the Horn.
- **Great Zimbabwe** — Height of the Zimbabwe Plateau civilization (c. 1220-1450). Massive stone enclosures, cattle wealth, gold trade with the Swahili Coast. Peak population perhaps 10,000-20,000 [Speculative].
- **Kilwa Sultanate** — Dominates the southern Swahili Coast. Sultan al-Hasan ibn Sulaiman builds Husuni Kubwa (c. 1310-1333). Controls gold trade from Sofala (Zimbabwe Plateau connection).
- **Benin Kingdom** — Emerges as a major forest-zone power. Begins developing the bronze-casting tradition and urban infrastructure that will peak in the next period.
- **Kanem-Bornu** — Kanem reaches its height under Mai Dunama Dibbalemi (c. 1210-1248). Bulala conflict begins; dynasty eventually relocates to Bornu.
- **Makuria** — Decline under Mamluk pressure. David of Makuria's defiance (1272) provokes devastating retaliation. Christian Nubia slowly disintegrates.
- **Hausa City-States** — Begin to develop as significant polities. Kano, Katsina, Zazzau (Zaria), and others emerge as urban centers.
- **Mapungubwe** — Precursor to Great Zimbabwe; flourishes c. 1075-1220, then declines as Great Zimbabwe rises.

**Parallel World Events:**
- Mongol Empire (1206-1368)
- Crusades continue (until 1291)
- Black Death in Europe and Middle East (1340s-1350s)
- Mamluk Sultanate in Egypt (1250-1517)
- Delhi Sultanate in India (1206-1526)
- Ming Dynasty in China (1368-1644)

---

### Late Period (~1400-1600 CE)

**Active Major Polities:**
- **Songhai Empire** — Sunni Ali (r. 1464-1492) conquers Timbuktu and Djenne, building the largest West African empire. Askia Muhammad I (r. 1493-1528) reforms administration and patronizes Islam. Destroyed by Moroccan invasion at Tondibi (1591).
- **Benin Kingdom** — Golden age under Ewuare the Great (c. 1440-1473), Ozolua (c. 1480-1504), and Esigie (c. 1504-1550). Bronze-casting reaches its peak. Portuguese contact begins (1480s).
- **Solomonic Ethiopia** — Zara Yaqob (r. 1434-1468) centralizes power. Ahmad Gragn's devastating jihad (1529-1543) nearly destroys the Christian kingdom. Portuguese intervention tips the balance.
- **Kongo Kingdom** — Portuguese arrival (1483). Nzinga a Nkuwu converts to Christianity (1491). Afonso I (r. 1509-1542) attempts to modernize through Portuguese alliance. Slave trade devastation begins.
- **Mutapa Empire** — Successor to Great Zimbabwe on the northern Zimbabwe Plateau. Portuguese penetrate the interior seeking gold. Changamire Dombo's revolt (c. 1490) creates the Rozvi Empire.
- **Hausa States** — Muhammad Rumfa reforms Kano (r. 1463-1499). Queen Amina's campaigns from Zazzau (c. 1560s-1590s). Growing Islamic scholarly culture.
- **Kanem-Bornu** — Bornu phase of the empire. Idris Alooma (r. c. 1564-1596) introduces firearms and reforms administration.
- **Swahili Coast** — Portuguese disruption. Kilwa sacked (1505), Mombasa attacked repeatedly. The Indian Ocean trade system is permanently altered. Swahili cities decline as autonomous powers.
- **Adal Sultanate** — Ahmad Gragn's jihad against Ethiopia represents the height of Muslim power in the Horn.

**Parallel World Events:**
- Portuguese Age of Exploration (1415+)
- Fall of Constantinople (1453)
- Columbus reaches the Americas (1492)
- Ottoman Empire at its height (Suleiman, r. 1520-1566)
- Mughal Empire in India (1526+)
- Reformation in Europe (1517+)
