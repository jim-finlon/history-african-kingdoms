## By Theme

### Trade Networks
- Trans-Saharan trade (gold, salt, slaves, textiles)
- Indian Ocean maritime commerce (gold, ivory, porcelain, textiles)
- Nile corridor trade
- Red Sea littoral
- Internal African exchange networks (iron, cattle, kola nuts)

### State Formation
- Sahel empires: Ghana → Mali → Songhai succession
- Forest kingdoms: Ife → Benin → Oyo progression
- Zimbabwe Plateau: Mapungubwe → Great Zimbabwe → Mutapa → Rozvi
- Ethiopian highlands: Aksum → Zagwe → Solomonic dynasty
- Swahili Coast: merchant city-states and sultanates

### Religion
- Indigenous African religions (ancestor veneration, spirit world, divination)
- Islam's spread across West Africa and the Swahili Coast
- Ethiopian Christianity (monophysite tradition)
- Nubian Christianity (Coptic connections)
- Religious syncretism and coexistence

### Intellectual and Cultural Traditions
- Timbuktu scholarly tradition and manuscript culture
- Oral literature (Sundiata epic, Dausi, clan genealogies)
- Benin bronze-casting tradition
- Ife terracotta and bronze sculpture
- Rock-hewn churches of Lalibela
- Swahili poetry and maritime culture

## Chronological Periods

| Period | Dates | Key Developments |
|--------|-------|------------------|
| **Late Antiquity / Early Period** | ~300-700 CE | Aksum's height, Nubian Christian kingdoms, early Swahili settlements, Ghana Empire's emergence |
| **Middle Period** | ~700-1100 CE | Ghana Empire's apogee, Islam's spread in West Africa, Swahili Coast urbanization, Kanem founded |
| **High Period** | ~1100-1400 CE | Mali Empire, Great Zimbabwe, Kilwa sultanate, Ethiopian Solomonic restoration, Benin's rise |
| **Late Period** | ~1400-1600 CE | Songhai Empire, Kongo kingdom, Portuguese contact, Benin's golden age, Swahili Coast disruption |

See [[00-Reference/Chronological-Framework]] for detailed chronological overlap chart.
